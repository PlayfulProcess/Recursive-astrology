# The Observatory replan — grammars of the sky (July 2026)

Written after reading the builder's books (`recursive-books-private`: *The 69%/31%*,
*Axiom Beneath the Ground*, *Grammars of the Living World*) at the builder's direction:
*respect different ways of knowing; ancient civilizations knew Sirius without telescopes;
don't create the duality "scientific knowledge is true" — we can only falsify hypotheses
with science, never the opposite; the equivalent of tarot's divination course is here a
philosophy of science one.* This supersedes the astro section of the family replan in
recursive-tarot/docs/plan/FAMILY-REPLAN-2026-07.md.

## 0. The correction this replan makes

The previous plan had three registers: "the referents (the sky as it physically is)," the
traditions, and the practice. That framing smuggled in exactly the hierarchy the builder
refuses: it made the measured sky the *base layer* and the traditions *lenses over it* —
science as the truth, everything else as commentary.

The corrected frame, in the builder's own vocabulary: **everything here is a grammar** —
a finite, coherent set of elements generating an infinite range of appropriate responses,
judged not by "is it true?" but by the three-filter test: *is it useful, does it fit the
data, is it compassionate.* The measured sky is one more grammar — the one that
systematically tries to falsify its own claims, which makes it unmatched at a specific
kind of question and silent on meaning. Mesopotamian omen-craft, the Sothic calendar,
songline astronomy, Hellenistic horoscopy, Jyotiṣa, and modern astrophysics are not
rungs on a ladder toward truth. They are different grammars of the same sky, each
producing real knowledge and real relationship in its own field. Falsifiable claims and
relational/axiomatic ones are different *kinds* of claim, not a hierarchy of true over
false ("the value of truth is an axiom, not a datum" — the filters cannot bootstrap
themselves).

Two disciplines guard the frame from both sides:

- **No scientism:** science's own criteria rest on chosen axioms; the perceptible world
  is a sliver ("the unknowable is not the same as the nonexistent"); humility about what
  instruments cannot reach is the *scientific* position, not a concession.
- **No romanticizing:** old knowledge is not automatically wisdom. The Dogon/Sirius-B
  story — beloved evidence of impossible ancient knowledge, most plausibly a modern
  contamination — gets told *as* the cautionary tale. Discernment about which old
  knowledge still functions belongs to practicing communities, held with humility.

## 1. The grammars of the sky (the data architecture, corrected)

Same machinery as before — shared item ids, the iching book-rail move — with the
privilege removed. Grammar families, all peers in the viewer:

- **`sky-as-measured`** (new, Phase B): planets, bright stars, constellations-as-regions
  as astronomy currently holds them; NASA public-domain imagery (`nasa_image_search`
  license-checks); discovery history in Research notes. Presented as *the measuring
  grammar* — its powers (falsification, prediction of positions) and its silences
  (meaning, value) stated on the tin.
- **The tradition grammars** (15 built): Mesopotamian → Ptolemy → Jyotiṣa → Lilly →
  Alan Leo → canonical modern → Tarnas → Proctor's skeptics — each faithful in its own
  terms, per the house rule that already governs this repo.
- **The practice grammars** (3 casting sequences, more later): the relating itself.

Shared-id alignment lets a viewer hold Mars still and page through every grammar that has
something to say about Mars — omen tablet, temperament, dasha lord, archetype, iron-oxide
desert world — **without the page ranking them.** That unranked co-presence *is* the
thesis, rendered.

One deliberate echo of *Why One Tradition*: the site presents many grammars (that is its
historiographic job — variety), while the courses recommend inhabiting one long enough
for relationship (diversity). "A buffet has variety. An ecosystem has diversity." Both
sentences belong on the site.

## 2. The course: "How We Know the Sky" (the philosophy-of-science course)

Astro's counterpart of tarot's *How Humans Have Always Cast Lots* — same method
(research-first, dossier with ✔︎/○/◆ confidence, then prose in the builder's voice), with
the tarot repo's formatting guardrails. Outline for approval:

1. **The star that told the river.** Sirius/Sopdet and the Sothic calendar: Egypt
   predicted the Nile flood from a heliacal rising, with no optics, for millennia —
   precise, load-bearing, real knowledge held in a religious grammar where the
   astronomical and the sacred were never separate. Then the honest counterweight: the
   Dogon/Sirius-B legend as the chapter's caution — the flattening that romanticizes is
   still a flattening.
2. **The songline and the wayfinder.** Aboriginal star maps and oral records verified
   against sea-level events thousands of years back — accurate *because* carried in
   ritual, landscape, and story; Polynesian navigation; the Mayan Venus table. The
   ancestral norm: ecological-astronomical knowledge fused with ethical and cosmological
   instruction, not layered on top of it.
3. **The archive that became astronomy.** The Mesopotamian omen diaries — centuries of
   nightly observation motivated by meaning — became the longest observational dataset in
   history, the one Hipparchus and Ptolemy built on. The meaning-grammar *produced* the
   data-grammar. There is no clean origin story where science begins by expelling meaning.
4. **What science actually is.** Falsification and its asymmetry (a thousand
   confirmations prove nothing; one clean refutation ends a claim) — and its limits: the
   demarcation problem, underdetermination, the axioms under the method itself, the
   sliver we perceive. "Valid is not equal to adaptive"; falsifiable and relational
   claims are different kinds, and the course gives readers the tool to tell them apart
   without ranking traditions by it.
5. **The tests that were run.** Carlson in *Nature*, the time-twins studies, the
   Mars-effect saga — what was actually falsified (specific causal-predictive claims),
   what was never touchable (the relating, the axiom), and why "no one has shown it"
   is the honest strength of the claim — same calibration as the tarot deep dives.
6. **Holding both — the honest practitioner.** Fidelity over certainty; the three-filter
   test as the working rubric; gesture, not solution; and the closing exhibit —
   **precession**: the signs and the constellations have drifted ~24° apart, so "which
   sky is the real one?" is revealed as the wrong question. Different skies in different
   times; different grammars for each; relate, never obey.

## 3. The cosmological deck, reframed

*Cosmological beings* (working title) stays — black holes, actual stars, the CMB — but
its charter sentence now comes from the builder's own Ch30: **"There is almost certainly
more. What it is, we do not know. How to relate to it, we must learn."** The deck is a
first-generation act of relationship-building with objects no tradition owns yet: Scene
= the verified astrophysics; Symbol = an open invitation to relate (never a meaning
system); the project's own voice, flagged as such — a gesture, not a solution.

· **variety vs. diversity** · **the sliver** · **technology of relationship** ·

family creed (*read the sky to know yourself, not to be told your fate*). Plus the

`confidence`; name a school, not a living person; imageless over wrongly-imaged.
