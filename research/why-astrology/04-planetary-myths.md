## Method note

Direct WebFetch of Wikipedia, Wikipedia mirrors, and several primary-adjacent sites (SEP, plato.stanford.edu-style pages) returned 403 this session. Every claim below was cross-checked via WebSearch's live search-grounded synthesis (which itself pulls from and cites live pages), and where possible corroborated against **two or more independent sources** before being marked ✔. Single-source claims are marked ○. Anywhere ancient/modern scholarship itself disagrees (the clearest case: which Mesopotamian god maps to Saturn) it is marked ◆ and the disagreement is kept visible in the grammar text rather than resolved.

## Sun (planet-sun)

- **Greco-Roman — Helios → Apollo conflation.** ✔ Helios (Titan sun-god, four-horse chariot) is uncontroversial; ○ the specific claim that Apollo "absorbed" Helios's solar identity by the Hellenistic period without a formal myth-merger is corroborated across secondary sources (Greek Names info page) but not confirmed against a primary text this session.
  - [Greek Names — "Greek names of the planets"](https://www.greek-names.info/greek-names-of-the-planets/)
  - Wikipedia, "Selene" (cross-referenced Helios material) — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selene
- **Vedic — Sūrya.** ○ Seven-horse chariot, driver Aruṇa, horses named for the seven Vedic meters (chandas), Gāyatrī mantra (Ṛgveda 3.62.10) addressed to Sūrya-as-Savitṛ — corroborated across two independent pages.
  - [Wikipedia, "Surya"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surya)
  - [Budaya Bali — "God Surya and the 7 Horses"](https://budayabali.com/god-surya-and-the-7-horses-the-timekeeper-energy-controller-and-guardian-of-cosmic-balance)
- **Babylonian — Shamash/Utu.** ✔ Sun-god and god of justice, astral triad with Sin and Ishtar, Hammurabi's code claiming Shamash as source — corroborated across World History Encyclopedia and Britannica independently.
  - [World History Encyclopedia — "Utu-Shamash"](https://www.worldhistory.org/Utu-Shamash/)
  - [Britannica — "Shamash"](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Shamash)

## Moon (planet-moon)

- **Greco-Roman — Selene/Luna, blending with Artemis/Diana.** ○ Selene as Titan daughter of Hyperion/Theia, Roman Luna's Aventine temple, and the later (post-classical) blending with Artemis/Diana as originally-distinct figures — corroborated across Wikipedia and Theoi.
  - [Wikipedia, "Selene"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selene)
  - [Theoi Project — "Selene"](https://www.theoi.com/Titan/Selene.html)
- **Vedic — Chandra/Soma, 27 wives = nakṣatras.** ○ The Daksha-curse myth (favoritism toward Rohiṇī → wasting-sickness curse → softened into waxing/waning) and the Soma-as-Moon-vs-Soma-as-ritual-drink ambiguity are both corroborated, the latter explicitly flagged as textually contested by the source itself.
  - [Wikipedia, "Chandra"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chandra)
  - [Dharma Station — "The Myth of Chandra and His 27 Wives"](https://www.dharmastation.ca/post/the-myth-of-chandra-and-his-27-wives)
- **Babylonian — Nanna/Sin.** ○ Oldest attested Mesopotamian god (c. 3500 BCE), Ur ziggurat cult center, judge-of-the-dead role in the Ur III period, parentage (Enlil/Ninlil) — single strong source (World History Encyclopedia), content consistent with other reference material seen in search snippets but not independently cross-fetched this session.
  - [World History Encyclopedia — "Nanna"](https://www.worldhistory.org/Nanna/)

## Mercury (planet-mercury)

- **Greco-Roman — Hermes → Mercury.** ✔ Messenger/herald role, patron of travelers/merchants/thieves, Roman adoption by the 4th century BCE merging with Etruscan Turms, *merx* etymology — corroborated across Wikipedia and Britannica.
  - [Wikipedia, "Hermes"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermes)
  - [Britannica — "Mercury"](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Mercury-Roman-god)
- **Vedic — Budha.** ○ Born of Chandra's affair with Tārā (wife of Bṛhaspati), the Tārakāmaya war framing, marriage to Ilā and fathering Purūravas (founder of the lunar dynasty) — corroborated across two sources.
  - [Wikipedia, "Budha"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Budha)
  - [Exotic India — "Budha: God of Planet Mercury"](https://www.exoticindiaart.com/article/god-of-planet-mercury-budha/)
- **Babylonian — Nabu.** ○ Son of Marduk (from the Kassite period onward), Borsippa cult city with annual New Year procession to Babylon, near-equal status to Marduk in the Neo-Babylonian period, Tablet of Destinies — corroborated across two sources.
  - [Ancient Origins — "Nabu"](https://www.ancient-origins.net/myths-legends-africa/nabu-0014933)
  - [Wikipedia, "Nabu"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nabu)

## Venus (planet-venus)

- **Greco-Roman — Aphrodite/Venus.** ✔ The two competing birth accounts (Hesiod's sea-foam vs. Homer's Zeus-and-Dione parentage) are both well-attested and explicitly presented as alternative traditions, not reconciled, in the sources themselves. ○ The specific 3rd-century-BCE Livy-sourced dating of the formal Aphrodite/Venus identification (Venus Erycina cult import) is a single-source detail.
  - [Wikipedia, "Aphrodite"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aphrodite)
  - [Ancient-Greece.org — "Aphrodite (Venus)"](https://ancient-greece.org/mythology/aphrodite-venus/)
- **Vedic — Shukra.** ○ Son of sage Bhṛgu, guru to the asuras, Sanjīvanī Vidyā (revival-of-the-dead knowledge won from Śiva) used repeatedly in the deva–asura wars — corroborated across two sources.
  - [Wikipedia, "Shukra"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shukra)
  - [Mytho World — "Shukra — God of Venus and the Guru of Asuras"](https://www.mythoworld.com/shukra-god-of-venus-and-the-guru-of-asuras/)
- **Babylonian — Inanna/Ishtar.** ✔ Combination of love/war/political-power domains, oldest-attested-deity claim, eight-pointed star as the Venus (morning/evening star) identifier — corroborated across two sources. ◆ The specific reading of her underworld-descent myth as *mirroring* Venus's observed disappearance/reappearance cycle is presented here as an interpretive scholarly reading, not a claim the ancient texts state outright.
  - [World History Encyclopedia — "Ishtar"](https://www.worldhistory.org/ishtar/)
  - [Wikipedia, "Inanna"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inanna)

## Mars (planet-mars)

- **Greco-Roman — Ares/Mars.** ✔ Ares as Zeus/Hera's son, disliked and little-worshipped by the Greeks; Mars as a more important, more "ethical," agriculture-linked Roman figure, second only to Jupiter — corroborated across two independent sources with the Greek/Roman contrast explicitly discussed by both.
  - [GreekReporter — "Ares vs. Mars"](https://greekreporter.com/2025/08/18/ares-mars-greek-roman-gods-war/)
  - [World History Encyclopedia — "Ares"](https://www.worldhistory.org/Ares/)
- **Vedic — Mangala/Aṅgāraka.** ○ Both competing origin myths (Śaiva: born of Śiva's sweat, raised by Bhūmi; Vaiṣṇava: tied to Varāha's boar-avatar piercing the Earth) are presented in the sources as genuinely parallel traditions, not one "correct" version.
  - [Wikipedia, "Mangala"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mangala)
  - [Exotic India — "About Mangala Grah"](https://www.exoticindiaart.com/article/about-mangala-grah/)
- **Babylonian — Nergal.** ✔ War/plague/death domains, agricultural-god origin in Kutha, the Nergal-and-Ereshkigal myth (joint underworld rule via marriage), noon-sun/dry-season association — corroborated across two sources.
  - [World History Encyclopedia — "Nergal"](https://www.worldhistory.org/Nergal/)
  - [Britannica — "Nergal"](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Nergal-Mesopotamian-deity)

## Jupiter (planet-jupiter)

- **Greco-Roman — Zeus/Jupiter.** ✔ Sky-and-thunder king role, Cronus/Rhea parentage, the shared Indo-European "sky father" root across Zeus/Jupiter/Dyaus — corroborated across two sources (the etymological point is standard comparative-mythology material).
  - [Wikipedia, "Zeus"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zeus)
  - [British Museum blog — "Gods and goddesses of the Greek and Roman pantheon"](https://www.britishmuseum.org/blog/gods-and-goddesses-greek-and-roman-pantheon)
- **Vedic — Bṛhaspati/Guru.** ○ Guru-to-the-devas role paralleling Shukra's guru-to-the-asuras role, Ṛgveda 4.50 appearance, "born from the first light" framing, Aṅgiras parentage in later texts — single strong source.
  - [Wikipedia, "Brihaspati"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brihaspati)
- **Babylonian — Marduk.** ✔ Enūma Eliš narrative (defeat of Tiamat, building heaven/earth from her body), elevation under Hammurabi (c. 1792 BCE) — this is standard, well-corroborated Assyriology. ○ The precise dating/mechanism of the later "Marduk = Jupiter" astral equation is less directly sourced this session (present in the earlier Babylonian-planets search but not independently re-verified against a dedicated astral-text source).
  - World History Encyclopedia, "Enuma Elish" (full text) — https://www.worldhistory.org/article/225/enuma-elish---the-babylonian-epic-of-creation---fu/
  - [EBSCO Research Starters — "Marduk"](https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/religion-and-philosophy/marduk)

## Saturn (planet-saturn)

- **Greco-Roman — Kronos/Saturn.** ✔ Castration of Uranus, child-swallowing/prophecy myth, Zeus's rescue by Rhea, and the harvest-god substrate beneath the horror-myth (Kronia festival ≈ Saturnalia) — corroborated across two sources, including the harvest-god reading being explicitly noted as scholarly consensus, not just folk etymology.
  - [Wikipedia, "Cronus"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cronus)
  - [Theoi Project — "Cronus"](https://www.theoi.com/Titan/TitanKronos.html)
- **Vedic — Shani.** ○ Son of Sūrya and Chhāyā (the shadow-double), karma/justice significator role, the "first glance caused an eclipse" birth detail — single strong source, internally consistent with what the Navagraha search returned independently.
  - [Wikipedia, "Shani"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shani)
  - [Exotic India — "All About Shani Dev"](https://www.exoticindiaart.com/article/all-about-shani-dev/)
- **Babylonian — Ninurta/Ninib.** ◆ **Explicitly contested, not just by us — by the sources themselves.** MUL.APIN-era texts (1st millennium) equate Saturn with Ninurta; but the search results themselves note "Ninurta was associated with the planet Saturn, **or perhaps** as offspring or an aspect of Saturn" and that under the earlier name **Ninib**, the same figure was sometimes read as a **solar** deity instead. This is flagged in the grammar item itself as the least-settled Mesopotamian mapping of the seven. The harvest-god parallel to Kronos is offered as the plausible reason the Greek-to-Babylonian identification chain (Kronos = Saturn = Ninurta) held, not as a certainty.
  - [Ancient Mesopotamia — "Ninurta"](https://ancientmesopotamia.org/ideology/ninurta)
  - [Wikipedia, "Ninurta"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ninurta)
